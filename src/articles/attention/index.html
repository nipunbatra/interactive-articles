Attention, Calculated

How does a Transformer know what a word means in context? The secret is "Self-Attention", a mechanism that lets words compare their "Queries" and "Keys" to calculate how much they should care about each other.

~15 min Deep Learning · Transformers · Attention
Part I

Queries, Keys, and Values

Let's look at a simple phrase: "The river bank".

In a Transformer, every word holds three vectors:

  • Query (Q): What am I looking for?
  • Key (K): What do I contain?
  • Value (V): If you care about me, here is what I contribute to your meaning.

Let's focus on the word "bank". It wants to know if it's a financial institution or the side of a river. It broadcasts its Query vector ($Q_{\\text{bank}}$) to compare against the Key vectors ($K$) of all other words.

Part II

The Dot Product (Raw Scores)

To calculate how much "bank" cares about "river", we take the Dot Product of their vectors. If the Query and Key point in the same direction, the score is high. If they are orthogonal (90 degrees), the score is zero. If they point in opposite directions, the score is negative.

Word Dot Product ($Q_{\\text{bank}} \\cdot K_{\\text{word}}$)
Part III

Softmax (Attention Weights)

Raw dot products can be any number (positive or negative). We want them to act like percentages (probabilities) that add up to 100%. To do this, we push the raw scores through a Softmax function.

Softmax aggressively rewards the highest score, turning the "winning" word into the dominant focus of attention.

Word Raw Score Attention Weight (Softmax)
Part IV

The Final Contextual Meaning

Now that "bank" has calculated its attention weights (e.g., 90% "river", 8% "bank", 2% "The"), it builds its new contextual meaning by mixing together the Value ($V$) vectors of all the words according to those weights.

Final Takeaway
Before Attention, "bank" was just an isolated token. After Attention, its new vector represents "a bank, specifically in the context of a river." This is how Transformers build deep, contextual understanding without reading left-to-right!
