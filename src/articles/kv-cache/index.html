The KV-Cache, Made Concrete

Every token an LLM generates re-uses every key and value from previous tokens. Caching them is mandatory — and the cache quickly dwarfs the weights. This is why "LLM inference is memory-bound, not compute-bound."

~11 min Inference · LLM systems
Step 1

The formula

Per-token KV bytes $= 2 \cdot L \cdot n_{kv} \cdot d_h \cdot p$ where $L$ is layers, $n_{kv}$ is KV-heads, $d_h$ is head dim, $p$ is dtype size.

For Llama-3-70B at FP16 with GQA(8) and context $32\text{k}$: $\approx 5\,\text{GB}$ per concurrent user. Run 16 users in parallel and the cache alone needs 80GB. The weights are already there.

Step 2

The three escape hatches

  • GQA · share KV across query heads. 8× reduction with negligible quality drop.
  • PagedAttention · allocate KV in fixed pages. No contiguous-buffer fragmentation; vLLM's secret.
  • INT8 KV-cache · halve bytes. Often free; sometimes a small quality nudge.
