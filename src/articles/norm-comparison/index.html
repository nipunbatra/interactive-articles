BatchNorm vs LayerNorm vs RMSNorm

Three normalizations. Same equation shape: subtract a mean, divide by a std (RMSNorm skips the mean). The only difference is which slice of the tensor the statistics come from. That one design choice is why BN dominated CNNs and LN dominates Transformers.

~12 min Regularization · Normalization
Step 1

One equation, three slices

All three follow: $\hat x = \gamma \cdot (x - \mu)/\sigma + \beta$.

  • BatchNorm · $\mu, \sigma$ from each channel, across batch + spatial.
  • LayerNorm · $\mu, \sigma$ from each example, across channels.
  • RMSNorm · like LN but skip the mean: $\hat x = \gamma \cdot x / \text{RMS}(x)$.

Step 2

Why each wins where it wins

  • CNNs · BN. Channels are repeated detectors; batch + spatial is a lot of samples.
  • Transformers · LN/RMSNorm. Sequences vary in length; no batch dependence is essential.
  • 2024+ · RMSNorm wins. One fewer parameter, similar accuracy, $\sim$5% faster.
