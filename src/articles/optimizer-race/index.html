The Optimizer Race

Deep learning models are trained by rolling down a hill (a "loss landscape"). Let's see why standard gradient descent gets stuck, and how Momentum and Adam were designed to fix it.

~15 min Deep Learning · Optimization · SGD
Part I

The Landscape

Imagine training a neural network with two parameters ($x$ and $y$). The height of the terrain is the loss (error). Our goal is to reach the lowest point (the minimum).

Below is a classic "ravine" optimization problem (similar to Beale's function or Rosenbrock's valley). The ravine slopes gently towards the minimum, but its sides are incredibly steep.

Part II

Why SGD struggles

Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) simply points directly downhill at every step. In a ravine, downhill points across the walls, not along the floor. So SGD spends all its energy bouncing violently wall-to-wall, making agonizingly slow progress toward the minimum.

Part III

Momentum: Adding Mass

Momentum remembers the direction of previous steps. It acts like a heavy ball rolling down the hill. The bouncing wall-to-wall movements cancel each other out, while the consistent push down the ravine floor builds up speed.

Part IV

Adam: Smart Scaling

Adam (Adaptive Moment Estimation) goes a step further. It not only keeps momentum, but it also measures how steep the gradients are in each direction. It dampens the steps along the steep walls and boosts the steps along the flat floor, creating a smooth, direct path to the minimum.
